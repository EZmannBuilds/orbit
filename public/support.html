    Signing in

    "Email or password did not match."

    This message is the same whether the password was wrong or no account exists for that address. That is deliberate — a different message would let anyone test which email addresses have Orbit Axis accounts. Check the address for typos, then try a password reset.

    Resetting your password

    1. On the sign-in screen, type your email address in the Email box.
    2. Choose Forgot your password?
    3. You will always see the same confirmation, whether or not an account exists — again, so the page cannot be used to discover who has one.
    4. Open the link in the email and choose a new password.
    5. You will be returned to sign-in. This is intended: a reset link should not also hand over a signed-in session.

    Reset links are single-use and expire. If yours says it has expired or already been used, request a new one. If the email has not arrived after a few minutes, check your spam folder.

    Email verification

    If your Orbit Axis account requires email confirmation, you will see a message saying so after signing up. Open the link in that email, then sign in. Until it is confirmed, signing in will not work.

    Creating a chart

    Orbit needs a birth date, a birth time if you know it, and a birthplace chosen from the suggestion list. Typing a city name without picking a suggestion will not work — Orbit needs the coordinates behind the name to calculate anything.

    If you do not know your birth time

    Choose Unknown birth time. Orbit will calculate planetary positions from a midday default and will withhold your houses, Rising sign, and Midheaven rather than guessing them. Your Moon may also be slightly approximate. Orbit tells you this on the chart instead of hiding it.

    A birth certificate is the most reliable source. A remembered time is usually fine for everything except a precise Rising sign.

    Saved charts

    • Your first chart is automatically named My Chart and set active.
    • Add more from the + next to the chart selector on Home, or Add Chart on Me.
    • The active chart decides which chart your daily reading is for.
    • Charts are stored on your account, so they follow you to any device you sign in from.

    Ask Orbit, and what it cannot do

    Ask Orbit answers from your calculated chart and the current sky. It is good at "what does my Saturn placement mean" and "what is happening in the sky today".

    It is not a doctor, therapist, lawyer, financial adviser, or emergency service. If you are in crisis or in danger, contact your local emergency services or a crisis line rather than an astrology app.

    Readings are for reflection and entertainment. They can be wrong, generic, or simply not resonate — that is normal and is not a fault to report.

    Deleting your account

    More → Account → Delete account, then type DELETE. It is permanent and immediate, and it cannot be undone or recovered — including by support. See how account deletion works.

    Privacy requests

    Reporting a security problem

    Security reports are welcome and taken seriously. Email not yet published with what you found and how to reproduce it.

    Please do not test against other people's accounts or real user data, and please give a reasonable chance to fix an issue before disclosing it publicly.

    Common errors

    What you seeWhat it means
    "Sign-in required" or you are returned to sign-inYour session ended. Sign in again.
    "Session expired. Please sign in again."Your session could not be renewed. Sign in again; report it if it keeps happening quickly.
    "Select the matching birthplace."A place name was typed but not chosen from the list. Pick a suggestion.
    "Too many requests."A safety limit. Wait a minute and try again.
    "This reset link has expired or has already been used."Reset links work once. Request a new one.
    Charts or readings will not loadUsually a temporary connection problem. Retry; if it persists, report it with the request id.

    Sending a useful error report

    When Orbit shows an error it usually includes a reference — a short random code identifying that one request. Including it lets the problem be traced exactly.

    The reference identifies a request, not you. It is safe to share, and it contains none of your birth details.

    A good report has: what you were doing, what you expected, what happened, and the reference if there was one.
